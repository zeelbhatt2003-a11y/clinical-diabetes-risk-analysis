# Exploratory Data Analysis

## Background

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) was performed to understand the distribution, central tendency, and variability of the clinical variables prior to inferential statistical analysis.

---

## Objective

To summarize the characteristics of the dataset and identify potential patterns, outliers, and anomalies.

---

## Methods

Descriptive statistics were generated in Jamovi for the following variables:

- Pregnancies
- Glucose
- Blood Pressure
- Skin Thickness
- Insulin
- BMI
- Diabetes Pedigree Function
- Age
- Outcome

Histograms and boxplots were also generated to visually assess the distribution of selected variables.

---

## Descriptive Statistics

The dataset consisted of **768 participants**.

Several variables demonstrated reasonable distributions, while selected clinical variables contained physiologically implausible zero values that were identified during the data quality assessment.

Summary observations included:

- Mean participant age: **33.2 years**
- Mean glucose concentration: **121 mg/dL**
- Mean BMI: **31.99 kg/m²**
- Mean systolic blood pressure: **69 mmHg**
- Mean number of pregnancies: **3.85**

---

## Visual Assessment

Histograms and boxplots were examined for key variables.

Key observations included:

- Blood pressure demonstrated an approximately normal distribution.
- A visible spike at zero was observed for blood pressure, indicating implausible values previously identified during data quality assessment.
- Several variables contained mild outliers, which are expected in clinical datasets.

---

## Interpretation

The exploratory analysis demonstrated that the dataset was suitable for statistical modelling while highlighting several data quality concerns.

The visualizations provided useful insight into variable distributions and confirmed the need to consider implausible zero values during interpretation of the results.
